Chapter 1: Spiritual Life (Instructions 1–10)

The foundation of a godly life rests on a personal relationship with Christ, nourished through prayer, study, and corporate worship. This vertical alignment determines all horizontal conduct.

1. Believe in Jesus Christ (John 3:16)

The Mandate: Salvation is the foundation of eternal life. All other instruction flows from this new life.

The Example (Story): The Samaritan woman at the well believed Jesus was the Christ and immediately became an evangelist, spreading the word in her city. (John 4:7–29)

The Application: What is the primary testimony of your life since you believed? Are you actively sharing the joy of your salvation?

2. Pray without ceasing (1 Thessalonians 5:17)

The Mandate: Prayer is the essential act of dependence that aligns her will with God’s will.

The Example (Story): Hannah prayed so earnestly for a son that Eli thought she was drunk. Her persistence brought forth Samuel. (1 Samuel 1:10–20)

The Application: Where have you allowed yourself to become busy instead of dependent? Write down three specific areas you need to commit to unceasing prayer this week.

3. Study Scripture (2 Timothy 2:15)

The Mandate: God’s Word directs her steps, guards against deception, and furnishes her for all good works.

The Example (Story): Lois & Eunice instilled the Scriptures in Timothy from a child, providing the earliest foundation for his faith and ministry. (2 Timothy 1:5)

The Application: How much time are you intentionally setting aside to study, not just read, the Word? Is your study time leading to true spiritual direction?

4. Worship in spirit and truth (John 4:24)

The Mandate: God seeks sincerity; worship must come from the heart and be based on the truth of His Word, not ritual alone.

The Example (Story): Mary of Bethany poured costly spikenard oil on Jesus’ feet, demonstrating sincere, extravagant worship, valuing Him above earthly wealth. (John 12:3)

The Application: Identify one area of your life (time, talent, or treasure) that you are holding back from extravagant, sincere worship.

5. Bear fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22–23)

The Mandate: Fruit (love, joy, peace) is the tangible evidence of living faith and the Holy Ghost's presence.

The Example (Story): Dorcas (Tabitha) was known for her good works and charity, showing visible fruit that defined her life. (Acts 9:36)

The Application: Which fruit (love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance) is currently weakest in your life? Pray for the Spirit's strength in that specific area.

6. Train younger women (Titus 2:4)

The Mandate: Older women have a spiritual duty to guide younger generations in practical and righteous living.

The Example (Story): Naomi provided guidance, protection, and wisdom to Ruth, teaching her loyalty that led to redemption. (Ruth 1–3)

The Application: Who is one younger woman in your life (family, church, or work) whom you can intentionally mentor in a practical, godly skill this month?
