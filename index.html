I. Trust, Transparency, and Integrity (7 Points)

Reasoning: Honesty in small things builds trust for big things. A godly relationship requires complete transparency to thrive. Faithfulness in managing money is a measure of spiritual maturity.

  • Proverbs 31:11 (KJV): "The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her..."
  • Luke 16:10 (KJV): "He that is faithful in that which is least is faithful also in much..."
  • Titus 2:5 (KJV): "...keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed." (Relates to discretion/honesty)
II. Work Ethic and Diligence (9 Points)

Reasoning: Diligence is a hallmark of godly character. Laziness is condemned because it leads to poverty and dependency. Planning is preparing for the future harvest.

  • Proverbs 31:13, 15, 17, 19 (KJV): Describes her eager, willing, strong, and hands-on work ethic.
  • 2 Thessalonians 3:10 (KJV): "...if any would not work, neither should he eat." (The principle of contribution).
III. Prudence and Wisdom in Spending (9 Points)

Reasoning: A wise woman looks ahead and prepares, while a fool spends everything immediately. Prudence guards against debt and secures the home's future stability.

  • Proverbs 31:16 (KJV): "She considereth a field, and buyeth it..." (Shows careful consideration/research).
  • Proverbs 21:20 (KJV): "There is treasure to be desired and oil in the dwelling of the wise; but a foolish man spendeth it up."
  • 1 Timothy 2:9 (KJV): "...with sobriety..." (Relates to modest spending, avoiding vanity).
IV. Debt, Greed, and Temptation (6 Points)

Reasoning: We are warned against the love of money, which leads to destruction. Avoiding debt prevents us from becoming slaves to creditors. Manipulation is deceitful and ungodly.

  • Romans 13:8 (KJV): "Owe no man any thing, but to love one another..."
  • 1 Timothy 6:9-10 (KJV): Warns against the desire to be rich, which leads to temptation and sorrow.
V. Conduct and Atmosphere of the Home (6 Points)

Reasoning: The woman's attitude is foundational to the home's atmosphere. Nagging and brawling destroy peace and honor. Grace and humility build lasting relationships.

  • Proverbs 21:9 (KJV): "It is better to dwell in a corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman..."
  • Proverbs 31:23 (KJV): "Her husband is known in the gates, when he sitteth among the elders of the land." (Her conduct honors him).
VI. Generosity and Giving (4 Points)

Reasoning: We are called to be channels, not reservoirs, of blessing. Giving is a direct expression of faith and love for God and neighbor.

  • Acts 9:36 (KJV): Describes Dorcas as "full of good works and almsdeeds which she did."
  • 2 Corinthians 9:7 (KJV): "...for God loveth a cheerful giver."
VII. Alignment and Home Leadership (5 Points)

Reasoning: A godly woman respects the order established by God while fulfilling her vital role as manager of the household's affairs and resources.

  • 1 Timothy 5:14 (KJV): "...guide the house..."
  • Colossians 3:18 (KJV): "Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as it is fit in the Lord." (The framework for unity).
VIII. Words, Communication, and Legacy (4 Points)

Reasoning: Wise and kind words are crucial, especially under financial stress. Our current actions form the legacy our children will inherit and follow.

  • Proverbs 31:26 (KJV): "She openeth her mouth with wisdom; and in her tongue is the law of kindness."
  • Proverbs 31:28 (KJV): "Her children arise up, and call her blessed..."
